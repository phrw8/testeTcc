About the band Nirvana

Nirvana was an American rock band formed by vocalist and guitarist Kurt Cobain and bassist Krist Novoselic in Aberdeen in 1987,[1] which achieved great success in the Seattle grunge movement in the early 1990s. Nirvana, who stayed in the band the longest was Dave Grohl, who joined in 1990.In the late 1980s Nirvana established themselves as part of the Seattle grunge scene, releasing their first album, Bleach, on the independent record label Sub Pop in 1989.

Curiosities about the band

  1. Kurt Cobain and Krist Novoselic met in high school. However, Dave Grohl only joined Nirvana shortly before the recording of "Nevermind". Before that, the band had 5 or 6 drummers.
  2. The band's first single, “Love Buzz”, was released in November 1988 on the Seattle-based independent record label Sub Pop. The following month, the band began recording their debut album, “Bleach”.
  3. “Smells Like Teen Spirit” was considered one of the 20 best songs in history.
  4. Did you know that the famous boy on the cover of "Nevermind" was supposed to be a girl? Photographer Kirk Weddle took two photos, one with a boy and one with a girl, and he liked the photo he took of the girl better. However, the band's record company insisted that the boy be chosen.
  5. The song "Polly" was written in honor of a 14-year-old girl who was kidnapped, tortured and abused in Tacoma Washington in 1987.
  6. Kurt didn’t like Guns N’ Roses and turned down the invitation to open for the band at a show.
